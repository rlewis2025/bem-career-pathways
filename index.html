BEM Career Pathways

A pathway-first job-search guide for BEM students who enter the School of Business in sophomore spring and must manage two searches: first, a required career-related summer internship; second, a full-time search that converts internship evidence, concentration choices, and leadership experiences into a targeted post-graduation offer.

551BEM outcomes by function / industry, FDS 2018-2025
323reported accepted job titles
2searches: internship + full-time
Start here: BEM students run two connected searches
The undergraduate BEM search is different from the graduate program search. Students enter the business school in sophomore spring, use junior year to secure and prepare for a meaningful internship, then use senior year to convert internship evidence, concentration focus, alumni relationships, and employer research into full-time opportunities.
Sophomore springchoose pathway direction, build resume, begin employer research
Junior yearsecure internship and align it with BEM 389 requirements
Summercomplete 200+ hours over 5+ weeks and build portfolio proof
Senior yearconvert internship, network, and apply for full-time roles

Search 1: Summer internship

The internship search should begin with pathway exploration and employer research, then move quickly into networking, applications, interview preparation, and BEM internship approval. The objective is not just an internship; it is career evidence for the senior full-time search.

BEM 389200 hours minimumPortfolio proofSupervisor feedback

Search 2: Full-time job

The full-time search should be more targeted: use the internship, concentration, leadership experience, and job-title evidence to choose a primary pathway, a backup pathway, a target employer list, and a weekly outreach/application rhythm.

Return offer strategyAlumni outreachRole-specific storiesTargeted applications
Recommended student flow: choose one primary pathway and one adjacent pathway, review employers that have hired BEM students, identify job-title search terms, build missing skills, then run a weekly search plan in Handshake, 12Twenty, LinkedIn, Wake Network, and employer career sites.

Timing tracks for the BEM search
BEM students run two connected searches. Use the customized target and skill plan above, then choose the track below that matches where you are in the undergraduate timeline.
Search 1 · Junior-summer internship
Secure a meaningful internship that satisfies BEM 389, gives you supervisor feedback, and creates the evidence you will use in senior-year recruiting.
Search 2 · Senior full-time job
Convert internship deliverables, concentration choices, BEM coursework, leadership experiences, and alumni networking into a focused full-time offer strategy.
Junior-summer internship search. Your internship is not just a requirement — it is the evidence engine for the full-time search. Treat the internship search like a role-family experiment: select one primary pathway, one adjacent pathway, and roles that will produce deliverables you can explain in interviews.
Goal: enter junior fall with a clear internship direction, not a blank search.
  • Pick one primary pathway and one backup. Use BEM outcomes, concentrations, and job title cards to decide whether you are testing consulting, marketing, finance, analytics, client service, management, entrepreneurship, or sports/media.
  • Translate BEM 211, BEM 221, BEM 202, and BEM 287 into early search language. Build a resume that shows teamwork, customer insight, quantitative thinking, and professional readiness.
  • Create a 30-employer seed list. Include BEM historical employers, local/regional employers, family/personal-network leads, Wake alumni employers, Handshake postings, and companies aligned with your concentration.
Goal: build a real internship pipeline before spring pressure peaks.
  • Submit 5-8 targeted applications weekly. Prioritize roles that will produce analytical, client-facing, operational, financial, or marketing deliverables you can show later.
  • Send 5-7 outreach messages weekly. Use Wake Network, WFU LinkedIn alumni, WFUSB LinkedIn alumni, employer events, and previous interns to learn role expectations and timing.
  • Ask evidence-based questions. Ask what projects interns own, what tools they use, how feedback is given, and what makes an intern convert to a return offer or referral.
Goal: secure an approved internship before work begins and enter it with a portfolio plan.
  • Confirm BEM 389 requirements early. Validate duties, supervisor, dates, hours, learning outcomes, and approval requirements before work begins.
  • Prepare portfolio categories before day one. Track deliverables, feedback, metrics, decisions, teamwork moments, and stakeholder communication examples.
  • Plan weekly reflection. Every Friday, write down the problem, your action, tool or framework used, result, and what story it supports for the senior search.

Weekly internship-search rhythm

  • 5-8 targeted applications to roles aligned with your primary or adjacent pathway.
  • 5-7 alumni/employer outreach messages tied to active or upcoming applications.
  • One resume or LinkedIn update using BEM coursework, concentration language, or leadership evidence.
  • One interview practice rep: behavioral, role motivation, or employer-specific answer.

BEM 389 readiness checklist

  • Minimum 200 hours over at least five weeks.
  • Approval, paperwork, and orientation before work begins.
  • Defined responsibilities and intended learning outcomes.
  • Status reports, final reflection, and portfolio artifacts.
Senior full-time job search. Your full-time search should not restart from zero. Use the internship, BEM concentration, leadership, ethics, simulation, strategy, and portfolio evidence to prove you are ready for a specific business role family.
Goal: start senior fall with a clear target, updated resume, and evidence bank.
  • Turn internship work into 3-5 strong bullets. Use problem, action, tool/framework, stakeholder, and result language.
  • Choose a primary and adjacent role family. Do not apply to everything; use BEM job-title evidence and employer cards to focus.
  • Update LinkedIn and portfolio. Add internship projects, BEM concentration, leadership, and target skills.
Goal: build enough high-quality pipeline before November.
  • Submit 6-10 targeted applications weekly. Tie each application to one role family and one evidence story.
  • Pair priority applications with relationships. Contact alumni or employer representatives before or shortly after applying.
  • Use BEM 318, BEM 365, BEM 388, and concentration work as interview evidence. Prepare stories on leadership, ethics, business judgment, and simulated decision-making.
Goal: diagnose the funnel and make targeted changes, not random changes.
  • If applications are not converting: revise target roles, resume evidence, keywords, and referral volume.
  • If interviews are not converting: practice internship impact stories, leadership stories, and role-specific motivation with a coach.
  • Expand strategically. Add adjacent titles, regional employers, alumni-led firms, growth companies, nonprofits, sports/media, or entrepreneurial organizations only where your story still fits.

Weekly full-time-search rhythm

  • 6-10 applications aligned with primary or adjacent pathway.
  • 6-8 networking touches with alumni, recruiters, and internship contacts.
  • Two interview practice stories: one internship-impact story and one leadership/team story.
  • Friday pipeline review: applications, contacts, interviews, rejections, follow-ups, and next actions.

        Your BEM story
        A strong BEM story connects liberal arts preparation, business fundamentals, concentration choice, internship proof, leadership, and role-specific motivation.

        Internship search story

        “I entered BEM to build a broad business foundation and I am targeting a summer internship in [pathway] because I want to test my interest in [role family]. My coursework and project work have prepared me to contribute through [skill], [skill], and [skill].”

        Full-time search story

        “My BEM concentration, internship experience, and leadership work point me toward [pathway]. In my internship, I produced [deliverable/result], which showed me I am strongest when [strength]. I am now targeting [job title] roles at employers like [target employers].”

        Coach prompt: Ask students to bring one pathway target, two role titles, five employers, one internship/project example, and one alumni contact list to each career conversation.
        Coach corner
        Resources for career coaches using this guide in BEM appointments, workshops, career classes, internship preparation, and full-time search check-ins.
        Before the appointment
        Ask students to identify their primary BEM pathway, concentration direction, and current search type before the session. Have them arrive with 2–3 role titles, 5 target employers, one job or internship description, and their current Skill Builder role-family and priority-skill selections.
        During coaching
        Use the Pathways, Employer Detail Cards, Job Titles, and Job Search sections to ground the student in real BEM outcomes. For juniors, translate pathway interests into a BEM 389-ready internship plan. For seniors, translate internship and concentration evidence into a full-time search strategy.
        Workshop use
        This guide works as a standalone workshop resource. Walk students through pathway selection, job-title evidence, employer targeting, and the Skill Builder. Have them complete the internship or full-time search dropdown live and leave with a first target list and weekly action plan.
        Staying current
        Employer and role data should be refreshed annually after the FDS reporting cycle and reviewed semesterly for internship and full-time relevance. Update BEM pathway examples when new confirmed hires, recurring internship hosts, or employer-partner patterns emerge.
        Governance reminders
        Outcome pages should display only authorized and aggregated information. Obtain consent before using named student, alumni, or employer-specific examples. Do not imply that prior BEM employer pipelines guarantee future internships, interviews, or full-time offers.
        Key platforms
        Direct students to Handshake for current postings and employer events, 12Twenty for historical outcomes and salary/location patterns, Wake Network for alumni outreach, LinkedIn Learning through WFU for skill development, and the BEM internship resources for BEM 389 planning.
