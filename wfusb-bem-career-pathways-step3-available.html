BEM Career Pathways

A pathway-first job-search guide for BEM students who enter the School of Business in sophomore spring and must manage two searches: first, a required career-related summer internship; second, a full-time search that converts internship evidence, concentration choices, and leadership experiences into a targeted post-graduation offer.

Start here: BEM students run two connected searches
The undergraduate BEM search is different from the graduate program search. Students enter the business school in sophomore spring, use junior year to secure and prepare for a meaningful internship, then use senior year to convert internship evidence, concentration focus, alumni relationships, and employer research into full-time opportunities.
Sophomore springchoose pathway direction, build resume, begin employer research
Junior yearsecure internship and align it with BEM 389 requirements
Summercomplete 200+ hours over 5+ weeks and build portfolio proof
Senior yearconvert internship, network, and apply for full-time roles

Search 1: Summer internship

The internship search should begin with pathway exploration and employer research, then move quickly into networking, applications, interview preparation, and BEM internship approval. The objective is not just an internship; it is career evidence for the senior full-time search.

BEM 389200 hours minimumPortfolio proofSupervisor feedback

Search 2: Full-time job

The full-time search should be more targeted: use the internship, concentration, leadership experience, and job-title evidence to choose a primary pathway, a backup pathway, a target employer list, and a weekly outreach/application rhythm.

Return offer strategyAlumni outreachRole-specific storiesTargeted applications
Recommended student flow: choose one primary pathway and one adjacent pathway, review employers that have hired BEM students, identify job-title search terms, build missing skills, then run a weekly search plan in Handshake, 12Twenty, LinkedIn, Wake Network, and employer career sites.

Job Search
Choose the search you are pursuing. BEM students need two related but different strategies: first, a junior-summer internship search that satisfies BEM 389 and creates evidence; second, a senior full-time search that converts that evidence into an offer.
Use the dropdown to switch playbooks. Each plan is organized like a working search system: positioning, target list, weekly rhythm, networking, applications, interviews, resources, and escalation steps.
Internship search objective: secure a meaningful, career-related summer internship that meets BEM 389 requirements, produces supervisor feedback, and gives you concrete work samples and stories for your senior full-time search. No internship credit is granted for work already in progress or completed, so approval timing matters.

Phase 1 · Sophomore spring

Pick a search direction before the market does it for you

Choose one primary pathway and one adjacent pathway. Build a short story that links your BEM interest, concentration option, coursework, campus leadership, and the kind of internship you want to test.

Phase 2 · Early junior fall

Build a 40-employer internship list

Start with BEM historical employers, then add organizations from Handshake, 12Twenty, Wake Network, employer events, family/campus networks, local/regional employers, and companies aligned with your concentration.

Phase 3 · Junior fall to spring

Network before and after applying

Contact alumni, recruiters, previous interns, and hiring managers with questions about internship projects, timing, team needs, and what strong undergraduate candidates show in interviews.

Phase 4 · Offer to approval

Confirm BEM 389 fit

Once you have an offer or serious lead, confirm duties, supervision, learning outcomes, hours, dates, and approval requirements before work begins.

Weekly internship-search rhythm

  • Applications: submit 5–8 targeted internship applications across Handshake, 12Twenty, LinkedIn, and employer career sites.
  • Networking: send 5–7 outreach messages to alumni, previous interns, employer contacts, and personal-network leads.
  • Events: attend one employer event, career fair, coffee chat, MRE program, or alumni conversation whenever available.
  • Follow-up: update every contact and application with status, next step, deadline, and follow-up date.
  • Interview prep: rehearse one behavioral story, one pathway-interest story, and one “why this employer” answer each week.

BEM 389 readiness checklist

  • Accepted BEM major; complete required School of Business credits, including BEM 211 and BEM 287.
  • Internship can support at least 200 hours over at least five weeks.
  • Responsibilities and learning outcomes are defined with the supervisor and internship advisor.
  • Approval paperwork is submitted and internship orientation is completed before work starts.
  • Status reports, final reflection, supervisor evaluation, and professional internship portfolio are planned from the beginning.

Internship target-list formula

Build a balanced list so you are not dependent on one channel.

10 proven BEM employers10 pathway employers10 local/regional targets5 alumni referrals5 stretch brands

Internship outreach message structure

1. Wake/BEM connection. 2. One sentence on your pathway interest. 3. One specific question. 4. Ask for 15 minutes. 5. Follow up with a thank-you and application update.

What counts as portfolio proof

Project brief, analysis, campaign recap, process map, customer research notes, dashboard, presentation, financial model, market map, supervisor quote, or reflection showing what you learned and how you contributed.

Sophomore spring
Explore and position. Learn BEM pathways, meet your coach, draft resume/LinkedIn, attend events, identify concentration interests, and start 10–15 informational conversations.
Summer before junior year
Build proof early. Add a work, volunteer, project, or leadership example; complete LinkedIn Learning modules in Excel, analytics, marketing, consulting, or project management; update your story.
Junior fall
Open the funnel. Apply early to structured internship programs, consulting/finance/marketing roles, and alumni-connected employers. Attend employer events and track every lead.
Junior spring
Convert and approve. Continue rolling applications, interview, evaluate offers, confirm BEM 389 eligibility, submit approval materials, and plan learning outcomes before work begins.
Summer internship
Perform and document. Build portfolio evidence weekly, ask for feedback, save non-confidential artifacts, document outcomes, and ask about full-time pathways or referrals.
  • Consulting / analyst: structured problem solving, teamwork, business judgment, and a simple case or scenario.
  • Marketing / sales: customer understanding, brand interest, communication, campaign thinking, and relationship skills.
  • Finance: Excel comfort, finance coursework, market awareness, attention to detail, and why financial services.
  • Analytics / insights: Excel, data interpretation, dashboard/storytelling examples, and curiosity about business questions.
  • Management / rotational: leadership, process improvement, adaptability, and cross-functional interest.
  • Entrepreneurship: ownership, ambiguity, customer discovery, resourcefulness, and examples of initiative.

For unpaid or low-paid internships, investigate the BEM Summer Work Fellowship. For qualified internships outside the student's country of residence, investigate the Coumantaros Global Exploration Internship Fund. Funding decisions may require an offer in hand, compensation details, projected living expenses, and a clear connection to the BEM internship requirement.

Full-time search objective: use your internship, concentration, BEM coursework, leadership experience, and alumni network to run a more focused senior-year search. The goal is not more applications; the goal is a stronger fit between your evidence, your target role, and the employers you pursue.

Phase 3 · Fall recruiting

Pair every application with a relationship

Use Wake Network, LinkedIn, employer events, and prior internship contacts. Your best application is usually the one that has a human conversation attached to it.

Phase 4 · Spring escalation

Audit and expand the funnel

If interviews are not converting, diagnose: target fit, resume evidence, referral volume, interview performance, or geography. Then widen into adjacent titles and employers.

Weekly full-time-search rhythm

  • Applications: submit 6–10 targeted applications aligned with your primary or adjacent pathway.
  • Networking: send 6–8 outreach messages, prioritizing alumni and contacts at employers where you applied or plan to apply.
  • Resume tailoring: customize role language, internship evidence, skill keywords, and measurable impact.
  • Interview reps: rehearse two stories weekly: one internship-impact story and one teamwork/leadership story.
  • Pipeline review: every Friday, update applications, contacts, interviews, rejections, follow-ups, and next actions.

Full-time search assets

  • One role-specific resume for each pathway you are pursuing.
  • LinkedIn profile naming BEM, concentration, internship evidence, leadership, and target skills.
  • Target employer list using BEM historical employers plus current Handshake/12Twenty postings.
  • Portfolio or story bank with internship deliverables, class projects, leadership examples, and quantified outcomes.
  • Clear answer to: “Why this role, why this employer, why now?”

Tiered target-employer model

Tier 1: proven BEM employers. Tier 2: employers in your target function/industry. Tier 3: regional, growth-stage, nonprofit, sports/media, entrepreneurial, or location-specific options.

Referral-first sequence

For each priority role: find alumni or recruiter → ask informed question → apply → follow up with application context → request advice or referral if the conversation supports it.

Spring still-seeking reset

If no interviews by February, widen titles and employers. If interviews but no offers, prioritize mock interviews, story clarity, technical prep, and role motivation.

        Your BEM story
        A strong BEM story connects liberal arts preparation, business fundamentals, concentration choice, internship proof, leadership, and role-specific motivation.

        Internship search story

        “I entered BEM to build a broad business foundation and I am targeting a summer internship in [pathway] because I want to test my interest in [role family]. My coursework and project work have prepared me to contribute through [skill], [skill], and [skill].”

        Full-time search story

        “My BEM concentration, internship experience, and leadership work point me toward [pathway]. In my internship, I produced [deliverable/result], which showed me I am strongest when [strength]. I am now targeting [job title] roles at employers like [target employers].”

        Coach corner
        Resources for career coaches using this guide in BEM appointments, workshops, career classes, internship preparation, and full-time search check-ins.
        Before the appointment
        Ask students to identify their primary BEM pathway, concentration direction, and current search type before the session. Have them arrive with 2–3 role titles, 5 target employers, one job or internship description, and their current Skill Builder role-family and priority-skill selections.
        During coaching
        Use the Pathways, Employer Detail Cards, Job Titles, and Job Search sections to ground the student in real BEM outcomes. For juniors, translate pathway interests into a BEM 389-ready internship plan. For seniors, translate internship and concentration evidence into a full-time search strategy.
        Workshop use
        This guide works as a standalone workshop resource. Walk students through pathway selection, job-title evidence, employer targeting, and the Skill Builder. Have them complete the internship or full-time search dropdown live and leave with a first target list and weekly action plan.
        Staying current
        Employer and role data should be refreshed annually after the FDS reporting cycle and reviewed semesterly for internship and full-time relevance. Update BEM pathway examples when new confirmed hires, recurring internship hosts, or employer-partner patterns emerge.
        Governance reminders
        Outcome pages should display only authorized and aggregated information. Obtain consent before using named student, alumni, or employer-specific examples. Do not imply that prior BEM employer pipelines guarantee future internships, interviews, or full-time offers.
        Key platforms
        Direct students to Handshake for current postings and employer events, 12Twenty for historical outcomes and salary/location patterns, Wake Network for alumni outreach, LinkedIn Learning through WFU for skill development, and the BEM internship resources for BEM 389 planning.
